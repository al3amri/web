Global Water Distribution

  • Saltwater: 97% (oceans)
  • Freshwater: 3% (2% glaciers/ice caps, <1% accessible)

Hydrologic Cycle

Steps: Evaporation → Transpiration → Condensation → Precipitation → Infiltration/Runoff

Unique Properties

  • Hydrogen bonding creates high surface tension
  • Maximum density at 4°C (ice floats)

Exam Practice (10 Questions)

1. Calculate BOD with Initial DO 8.3 mg/L, Final DO 2.3 mg/L, Dilution 60

BOD = (8.3 - 2.3) × 60 = 360 mg/L

2. Explain the eutrophication process

Excess nutrients → Algae bloom → Oxygen depletion → Dead zone

3. Difference between point and nonpoint pollution sources

Point: Direct discharge from pipes. Nonpoint: Diffuse runoff

4. Name three stages of wastewater treatment

Primary, Secondary, Tertiary

5. What does COD measure?

Total oxidizable organic/inorganic matter using chemical oxidizers

6. Why does ice float on water?

Water reaches maximum density at 4°C, ice is less dense

7. What is the purpose of activated sludge?

Aerobic bacteria digest organic waste in secondary treatment

8. How does thermal pollution affect aquatic life?

Reduces dissolved oxygen, increases metabolic rates

9. What are DBPs in water treatment?

Disinfection By-Products formed during chlorination

10. How to remove permanent water hardness?

Ion exchange resins or chemical precipitation
